# Product

## Register

product

## Users

MAGGA serves Thai readers and Thai translators of furry manga and doujin works. Readers come for a focused, trustworthy reading experience with fast browsing, search, filtering, profile activity, comments, and a comfortable vertical reader on mobile and desktop. Translators and contributors need a clear submission path, dependable upload behavior, and a managed environment where their translated works can be presented without the surface feeling cheap, cluttered, or unsafe.

Admins and moderators use the dashboard to review submissions, manage manga metadata, comments, users, advertisements, authors, categories, tags, and policy-sensitive content. Their workflows need to feel efficient and controlled, but the public product identity should remain centered on Thai furry manga translation rather than on administration software.

## Product Purpose

MAGGA is a central hub for Thai-translated furry manga and doujin works. It exists to make discovering, reading, submitting, and managing translated furry manga feel organized, fast, and credible. Success means readers can find and enjoy works with low friction, translators can share their work through a clear pipeline, and the site maintains enough polish, moderation, and performance to feel like a dedicated community product rather than an ad-heavy archive.

## Brand Personality

MAGGA should feel focused, mature, and fan-culture native. The tone can be direct and energetic where it helps discovery, but it should avoid looking noisy, desperate, or generic. The brand should balance dark manga-reader immersion with enough editorial confidence to make translated works feel intentionally presented.

## Anti-references

MAGGA should not look like a manga site overloaded with ads, popups, confusing banners, or visual clutter. It should not look like a generic SaaS dashboard, especially on public reader and discovery surfaces. It should also avoid the untrustworthy visual language common to low-quality 18+ sites: aggressive colors, fake urgency, unreadable layouts, suspicious controls, and content presentation that makes the product feel unsafe or throwaway.

## Design Principles

1. Keep the work central. Covers, pages, authors, tags, and reading flow should carry the experience more than decorative UI.
2. Make mature content feel credible. The product can acknowledge its adult/fandom context without borrowing the cheap patterns of low-trust adult sites.
3. Serve readers first, contributors second, admins third. Public discovery and reading should stay frictionless; submission and moderation workflows should be clear but not dominate the product identity.
4. Prefer dense clarity over spectacle. MAGGA should support searching, filtering, reading, commenting, and managing content without turning every screen into a marketing surface.
5. Protect performance on constrained hosting. UI and workflow choices should respect Vercel Hobby limits, image-heavy pages, and mobile readers.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG A at minimum across public reading, discovery, account, submission, and admin workflows. Maintain usable contrast in the dark theme, clear focus states for keyboard navigation, readable Thai typography, stable mobile layouts, and non-blocking alternatives for hover-only interactions. Motion should stay subtle and should not be required to understand or complete a task.
